# Welcome to Tiro

Tiro is a local-first reading OS for the AI age. It saves the web pages and
email newsletters you care about as clean Markdown, enriches them with
AI-extracted metadata, and reasons across your whole library — daily digests,
trust analysis, and reading preferences it learns from you. Everything runs on
your own machine. You own your data.

## Saving things to read

- **From the web:** paste a URL into the inbox, or use the Chrome extension to
  save the page you're on with one click.
- **From your inbox:** connect Gmail and Tiro pulls in the newsletters you
  subscribe to, stripping tracking pixels and turning each one into a clean
  reading view.

Every saved article becomes a Markdown file with YAML frontmatter in your
library folder — readable and portable forever, with or without Tiro.

## Making sense of it all

Once you have a few articles saved, Tiro's intelligence layer goes to work:

- **Digests** synthesize what you saved into a short daily briefing.
- **Highlights and notes** you make while reading are stored as sidecar files
  right next to the article — never locked inside a database.
- **The knowledge graph and wiki** connect the people, companies, and ideas
  that recur across your reading.

## A note on privacy

Tiro is yours. The library lives in a folder on your computer. The only time
anything leaves your machine is when *you* ask an AI model to help — and you
choose which model, including local ones. There is no account, no cloud, and no
tracking.

Named after Marcus Tullius Tiro, Cicero's freedman and secretary, who invented
