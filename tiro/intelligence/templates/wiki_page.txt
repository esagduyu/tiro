## Task
Write the markdown BODY of this wiki page (or, if a prior version was supplied above,
rewrite/update it in place — merge new material into existing sections rather than
appending a new one for every article; compress, don't proliferate).

Rules — follow all of them:
1. **Cite every claim.** Every factual statement must carry a citation formatted as a
   wikilink: `[[stem|short label]]`, where `stem` is one of the exact citation stems
   given in the Source Articles section above and `short label` is a brief (a few
   words) human-readable gloss of what that source shows — never invent a stem, never
   cite a source that isn't listed above, and never omit the citation on a claim that
   traces back to a specific article.
2. **Name trust signals in prose, don't hide them in a footnote.** When a claim comes
   from a VIP source, a loved or liked article, or a disliked one, say so in the
   sentence itself — e.g. "your most-trusted source reports X [[stem|label]], while a
   source you disliked claims Y [[stem|label]]." Silence about trust is a defect: the
   reader should be able to tell, from the prose alone, how much weight a claim
   deserves.
3. **Compress.** This page is a synthesis, not a log of articles. Merge overlapping or
   redundant material from multiple sources into a single statement with multiple
   citations rather than restating the same fact once per source. When updating an
   existing page, fold new information into the existing structure — rewrite a
   paragraph, don't just tack a new one onto the end — and drop anything superseded or
   made redundant by newer, better sources.
4. **Stay dense and factual.** No filler, no throat-clearing, no "this page discusses…"
   preamble. Every sentence should carry information a reader could not get faster by
   skimming the article list themselves.
5. **Output the BODY only.** Do not include frontmatter (no `---` YAML block), do not
   wrap the output in code fences, and do not repeat the page title as a heading — the
   renderer already supplies the title from the page's own metadata. Start directly
   with content (an introductory paragraph, or a `##` subsection heading if the
   maintenance instructions call for structured sections).
